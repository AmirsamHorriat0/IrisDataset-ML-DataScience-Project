In[6]:
iris = load_iris()
df = pd.DataFrame(iris.data , columns = iris.feature_names)
df ['species'] = iris.target
df ['species'] = df['species'].map({0: 'setosa' , 1: 'versicolor' , 2: 'virgincia'})

summary_table = pd.DataFrame({
    'Feature' : df.columns,
    'Data Type' : df.dtypes.values,
    'Non-Null Count' :
df.notnull().sum().values,
    'Unique Values':
[df[col].nunique() for col in df.columns]    
})

print('\n Basic DataSet info : ')
print(summary_table)

display(df.head())

Numbers close to 1 or 1 are known as strong connection , Numbers close to -1 or -1 are known as strong reverse connection and numbers close to 0 or 0 are known as weak connection or no connection.

By the way you can change the color of this chart as example writing downn WINTER instead coolwarm.

Visualizing feature distribution across classes

plt.figure(figsize = (12,2))
for i , column in enumerate(df.columns[:-1]):
    plt.subplot(2 , 2 , i + 1)
    sns.violinplot(x = 'species' , y = column , data = df)
    plt.title(column)

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

The wider sections of the plot indicate higher data density.

If the two classes do not overlap , it means that the given features can help differentiate between the classes.

After all this analyzings now we can apply a simple model like KNN and test it out let's see

In[33]:
from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.preprocessing import StandardScaler 
from sklearn.neighbors import KNeighborsClassifier
from sklearn.metrics import accuracy_score , classification_report
In[35]:
x = df.drop(columns = ['species'])
y = df['species']

There is an important topic in machine learning called model evaluation, where we teach the model to use 80% of the data for learning and the remaining 20% of the data for testing using MAE, MSE or RMSE.

Ni=1N ypred yactual

In[40]:
x_train , x_test , y_train , y_test = train_test_split(x , y , test_size = 0.2 , random_state = 42 , stratify = y)
In[42]:
scaler = StandardScaler()
x_train = scaler.fit_transform(x_train)
x_test = scaler.transform(x_test)
In[44]:
knn = KNeighborsClassifier(n_neighbors = 3)
In[46]:
knn.fit(x_train , y_train)
Out[46]:
KNeighborsClassifier(n_neighbors=3)

In[48]:
y_pred = knn.predict(x_test)

accuracy = accuracy_score(y_test , y_pred)
